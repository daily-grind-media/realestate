Standards & Process

How We
Protect Quality

Premium creative work requires clearly defined expectations, efficient communication, and structured production. These practices protect the quality of your deliverables, keep projects on schedule, and ensure every engagement reflects the premium standard Daily Grind Media is built around.

I

Defined Deliverables

Every engagement includes exact deliverable counts and clearly listed exclusions — so you always know what is included and what falls outside the agreement. Vague phrases like "ongoing content" are never used.

II

Two Revision Rounds

Each major deliverable includes up to two rounds of revision. Additional revision rounds are billed at a stated rate. This protects production timelines and maintains the integrity of the creative process.

III

Consolidated Feedback

Client feedback must be consolidated into one round per review window. Fragmented or sequential notes prevent version control and delay timelines. One clear, complete round per deliverable keeps projects moving.

IV

Communication Standards

All project communication is managed through one agreed channel with a defined response window. A single communication structure keeps everything organized and accountable.

V

Change Orders

Any request that materially adds time, complexity, or recurring responsibility is quoted as a formal change order before work begins. No scope expansion happens without written approval.

VI

Monthly Deliverable Expiration

Unused monthly deliverables expire at the end of the service period unless otherwise agreed in writing. This ensures consistent production cadence and prevents backlog accumulation.

VII

Additional Production Days

All engagements include a defined production window. Additional shoot days, extended sessions, or expanded locations beyond the agreed scope are quoted separately before scheduling.

VIII

Additional Concepts

Each creative engagement is built around one approved concept direction. Requests for alternative concepts or additional versions are treated as separate scope and quoted accordingly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common
Questions

How quickly can we get started?▼
Most projects can begin within one to two weeks of a signed agreement and initial deposit. Monthly partnership clients typically begin with a discovery and strategy call in the first week, followed by their first production session in the second or third week. Timeline can vary depending on scheduling availability, project complexity, and how quickly assets and approvals are provided.
How many revisions are included?▼
Each major deliverable includes up to two rounds of revision. A revision round means one complete, consolidated set of notes submitted at the same time — not a series of sequential small requests. This structure keeps projects on schedule and ensures the creative quality remains consistent throughout. Additional revision rounds beyond the included two are available and billed at a flat rate.
Can packages be customized?▼
Yes. While the service guide presents defined tiers and offerings, most engagements are customized to some degree based on your market, goals, and production needs. During our discovery process, we identify the right combination of services for your specific situation and build a proposal around it. If you have a specific need not listed in the guide, we are happy to discuss it.
Can monthly partnerships be upgraded?▼
Yes. Monthly partnerships can be expanded or upgraded at the start of a new cycle. If your business grows, your team expands, or your content needs increase, we can adjust the scope of your partnership accordingly. Upgrades are structured as a new agreement rather than ad hoc additions mid-cycle, which keeps deliverables and expectations clear for both parties.
Do unused deliverables roll over?▼
Unused monthly deliverables expire at the end of the service period unless otherwise agreed in writing at the start of the engagement. This policy exists to maintain production consistency and prevent backlogs that compromise quality. If you anticipate needing more flexibility in scheduling, we can discuss how to structure your partnership to accommodate that in advance.
Can you travel outside San Diego?▼
Yes. We are available for travel to support clients outside of the San Diego area for both production and partnership engagements. Travel fees including transportation, accommodation, and time are quoted separately and included in any out-of-area proposal. For recurring partnerships, travel logistics are discussed and planned as part of the agreement structure.
Who owns the final media?▼
Upon receipt of final payment, clients receive full usage rights to all delivered media assets for agreed purposes including marketing, social media, websites, and listing presentations. Specific licensing terms are outlined in the project agreement. Raw files and unedited materials remain the property of Daily Grind Media unless otherwise negotiated and specified in writing.
How does billing work?▼
Project-based services require a deposit to confirm the booking, with the remaining balance due upon delivery of final assets. Monthly partnership clients are billed on the first of each month for that month's service cycle. All billing details, payment methods, and due dates are outlined in the client agreement before work begins. We do not begin production work without a signed agreement and confirmed deposit.
